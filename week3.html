Welcome

Hello and welcome to my little corner of the internet. i will be using this page to learn a little coding. I'm currently embarking on a #100DaysOfCode journey and I will be documenting this on here as well as use this as a practice piece.

With this being a practice piece it will likely go through quiete a few changes over time so I hope you can bare with me.

the urrent plan is to complete a Full stack developer course I'm doing on CodeCademy (which I do not count to the time for the 100 day challenge) and on the side practice what I learn on this block as well as on a a little project for tracking details about Sims in The Sims 4™. I know this might strike people as an odd choice of project but it is a franchise that has been close to my heart since it first released and I feel that there is a lot potential for me to practice a large number of technologies on it. Since I need to start with some layout work on that first I am starting my practice in this little blog.

For the moment this will only be about my coding journey, but I will see if I add more other content to it eventually.

Day 15: 18th July 2022

  1. Blog entry about work during sickness hiatus ☑

Today's Progress: Update on hiatus and summary of work during break

Thoughts: Phew it has been quite here hasn’t it. As mentioned in my one I was unfortunately struggling with my crowns and did not mange to consistently work on my coding things, but now I’m getting better I’m exited to pick it back up. I have not stopped working or learning completely though so I’m going to give you a little summary of what I have done.

I have continued some of the CodeCademy course work starting the JAvaScript chapter. This was mostly rekindling the knowledge on conditional statements and variables. And while I find myself often just think of if-blocks regarding conditionals I do believe switch-blocks are magical especially for more complex decision points, so I need to make sure I keep them in the back of my mind more.

For my little training project The Sims 4 Tracker I also made some nice progress. I did some design work and transferred the designs into html/css. All as previously planned in static just for getting it all up and looking as I like the actual meet behind will have to be turned dynamic when I am capable of doing that. I’m fairly proud of finally managing to get a good levee of responsiveness into the project I rewrote some of the work I previously done to get right and went with a mobile first approach. To hide and hide elements based on the available space is an achievement I was particularly happy about. Working with CSS grid worked like charm for this. The pages I finished can be seen below. So far I have finished the landing page, sign up, login and the dashboard. The dashboard still needs some correction alignments but it is almost where I want it to be. Here are some pictures of dash board design as planned and how it is currently looking on the writen page.

Today I found an interesting looking project on github called Rebus. This project wants to encourage people to start getting involved in open source project by given them an easy entry and explaining how contributing and working together works. Since I have never done this before I figured I will try and made a fork for me to get started with. If you are also looking to get more experience in that area I recommend that you have a look. I will let you know how I get on with that as well.

Links to work: Sims Tracker Rebus (open source project)
